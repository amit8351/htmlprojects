Ubud is the place to be if you’re looking to immerse in Bali’s rich cultural heritage and artistic scene. You can find numerous tourist spots like the Monkey Forest, Ubud Palace and Tegalalang Rice Terrace.

If you are interested in local art, you can also drop by the Ubud Art Market to get your hands on intricate knick-knacks and handmade trinkets!

Besides so many unique things to do and see in Ubud, you can also find unforgettable dining experiences, combining both gastronomical dishes and panoramic views. Here, you can expect breathtaking views of the mystical rainforest and paddy fields – a perfect escape from the bustle of the South!

1. With an infinity pool overlooking paddy fields  – Tis Cafe Ubud

Want to visit Tegalalang rice terraces but don’t want to walk in the heat? Enjoy the best of both worlds by heading down to Tis Cafe, nestled in the middle of the rice paddies, while sipping on tea under the cool shade.

Tis Cafe offers one of the best views overlooking the cascading rice terraces. What’s even better – you can take a refreshing dip (also for the ‘gram) in the on-site infinity pool!

Beyond just a pretty view, the cafe also serves up international cuisine, with dishes ranging from pizzas and burgers to local favourites like crispy duck!

P.S. If you’re looking at how to get here, Tis Cafe is located within the compound of Nau Ubud Villas!

2. It’s exquisitely luxurious with delectable food and unforgettable views – CasCades Restaurant

Viceroy Bali is perfectly situated in the Valley of the Kings. And one of the most gracious jewels in the Viceroy Bali is no doubt its award-winning, and stunning restaurant – CasCades.

With regular piano performances accompanying the evening birdsong, and sounds of the jungle mingling to form the most unforgettable harmony…

Along with exquisite fusion food featuring flashy, unique and innovative dishes boasting European and Asian influences.

You can be sure that an experience at CasCades, is just as unforgettable as its views of the stunning Petanu River gorge

Perched on the valley ridge, overlooking a serene infinity pool and the surrounding jungle. A tropical green oasis of trees and vines for as far as the eye can see.

To taste Bali in a whole new way, you have to try the Indonesian Degustation menu. This meticulously crafted 8-course extravaganza features traditional dishes with a modern twist. And did you know – Cascades regularly invites top international chefs who present their culinary skills through some amazing degustation menus!

‘Fusion’ even extends to the architecture and décor of CasCades. This luxurious open-air restaurant showcases a traditional Balinese thatched roof, and mysterious tropical jungle surroundings – while remaining fresh and contemporary.

CasCades is a treat for all your senses – don’t miss out and submerge yourself in this truly luxurious experience.

Ps: Top off the experience with a toast to your unforgettable night, with an abundant selection of delicious wines available!

3. Celebrate romance amidst nature – The Sayan House

This quaint little restaurant overlooking the Ayung River is a popular spot for private receptions, and we can see why.

On a day when you’re supposed to feel like you’re on top of the world, what could be better than literally being on top of the world?

The Sayan House will speak to the nature lover in you, with its signature view of forests, rice fields and the rushing waters of the sacred Ayung River below.

Enjoy a romantic dinner with your loved one while watching the sun set over the valley, or hang some fairy lights on the trees and watch the place come alive as a fairytale wedding setting.

Better yet, The Sayan House has some pretty amazing contemporary and unique Asian dishes up for grabs.

Try out their Crispy King Prawn & Avocado Maki, and the Salmon Carpaccio too! There are also fusion dishes like Babi Guling Sushi – if you’re fond of trying out new things, you won’t be disappointed.

4. Infinity views from this co-working space — Wild Air

Located near Monkey Forest in Central Ubud, Wild Air is situated in Kaamala Resort. Wanna dine with a view? The restaurant offers unparalleled views of the lush greenery, and even comes with an infinity pool for you and your travel buds to dip into on a hot day.

For the best pictures, be sure to get a shot of yourself on the floating mesh beds! This place also has the best views of the sunset, so make sure you head down before 5pm to secure good seats!

If you’re a digital nomad, you’ll be thrilled to know that they offer co-working spaces for you to get a productivity boost while overlooking the beautiful scenery.

Wild Air offers both Asian and Western menus, complemented with signature beverages that are specially created by their professional chefs. Expect to chow down on dishes like Lontong Sayur, fried rice and noodles, pasta, crispy duck, pork ribs and more!

5. The sparkling Ayung River and emerald verdant forest – Kubu and Sawah Terrance

Be spoilt for choice at the glitzy Mandapa, Ritz Carlton’s answer to Ubud.

Whether you’re a lover of soothing water scenes like the Ayung River, or adore fresh vibrant views of paddy fields and forest – Mandapa has them both.

Introducing Kubu, Mandapa’s signature bamboo restaurant that not only provides intimate dining with 9 private cocoons – but soothing views of the Ayung River too. This luxurious restaurant makes the list of one of the best restaurants to be, thanks to its incredible ambience and delicacies available.

Here, you’ll get to enjoy Mediterranean-European cuisine, with the melody of flowing water and a cooling tropical breeze. Only dinner is served here, so you’ll surely enjoy a dreamy night – perfect for the romantic date you’ve been waiting for.

If not, Sawah Terrace provides similarly gorgeous views showing off Ubud’s verdant paddy fields and forest.

More for those looking to dig into Balinese cuisine and other Asian favourites, along with organic farm-to-table healthy offerings – feel free to head over for breakfast, lunch or dinner.

This all-day-dining restaurant will satisfy you at every meal, and keep your senses feeling fresh with vibrant green views.

Fun fact: Looking at green colours is a great way to soothe any tired eyes!

10. Tropical Paradise – Kepitu

Drift away into pure bliss with the incredible floating sky roof deck at the Kepitu restaurant.

A spectacular 180-degree view of the surrounding jungle awaits you. And a cascading waterfall completes this tropical paradise – what more could you ask for?

Kepitu is renowned for its Indonesian cuisine, but other Asian and Western influences also shine through. An onsite bakery also ensures that you get the freshest baked goods, making breakfast extra special.

Enjoy a delicious light lunch menu full of refreshing salads, and beautifully crafted sandwiches and sweet treats.

The Kayon Resort is famous for its personal touches, and this extends even to the restaurant. Expect exceptional, friendly and professional service, as you enjoy a faultless and delicious meal.

An illuminated stone Kumbu Karna Statue on the roof deck is a reminder of the diverse Balinese culture and looks spectacular with the jungle backdrop.

This hidden gem of a restaurant will transport you to another mysterious world – a mystical tropical utopia.

11. Calming Dining Experience in T’dung Restaurant

Bali never ceases to melt your heart with serene views. T’dung restaurant is a little jewel in Kenran Resort, located in the deep forest of Tegallalang.

The azure colour of its tiered infinity pools heightens your dining views in contrast to the emerald forest backdrop.

There’s also a separate whirlpool for kids to enjoy.

Elevated above the Petanu River, T’dung features an open bar on its rooftop for guests to enjoy the mesmerizing view of the jungle. With a minimum spend of Rp.150,000 (which includes towel rental and Rp. 100,000 F&B credits), you are free to dine, lounge and swim here, and you’ll have The cabanas and sunken sunlounger are available for reservation!

If you are looking for a chill area away from the crowds, T’dung restaurant is perfect as the overall vibe of the place is calm and quiet. Even when there are many people, the restaurant is open and airy so you still have a sense of personal space dining here.

After a hearty meal of Thai Western fusion dishes, you can indulge in the view from the pool lounge area. While you are at it, simply have some fun photo ops as there are so many instagrammable spots here, such as a heart-shaped deck that extends out to the forest.

This restaurant is definitely made to be remembered!

12. Hidden breakfast gem in Ubud – Section 9

We stumbled upon this hidden gem just a short drive away from Ubud’s city centre – a quiet little cafe – a refreshing break from the bustle and heavy traffic of Jalan Raya Ubud.

Over here, you can choose to sit indoors, or outdoors right next to a vast field of paddy fields. It was quite comfortable outdoors and only slightly warm, even though we were there at around 10am (and as fellow Southeast Asians, we’re definitely not a fan of the tropical heat!).

We felt super chill and comfy, not to mention the cafe has a beautiful earth-tone decor – so it’s IG-worthy in almost every corner.

Here, they serve both Western, Mexican fusion and Indonesian breakfasts and snacks (their quesadillas were delicious!). They also have good options for desserts and coffee too! If you’re looking for something healthy, there’s lots of options to choose from – from salads, poke bowls, to smoothies and more.

Their wifi is also pretty strong – so fellow digital nomads, feel free to get some work done after brekkie. After all, isn’t working by the beach or in the middle of rice paddies the dream in Bali?!

17. Eat amongst lush jungle and calm winds – Lembah Ayung Restaurant at Pramana Watu Kurung

Lush green jungle surround this restaurant, a sheer drop is what lies beneath you as you dine. Facing the west, this is one of the best places in Ubud to watch the sunset!

Situated on top of a hill, as close to the clouds as you can get, the air is fresh, and the food even fresher!

Being open air, you can enjoy the breeze which may come at you from any side, listen to the rustling of leaves, and smell the lively odours of the jungle. There’s no better way to fully immerse yourself in the rainforest!

21. The Moonlit Paradise – Indus Restaurant
